How to Teach Kids to Swim

Many parents want to help their children become safe and comfortable in the water. Teaching kids to swim starts with patience, trust, and simple skills that build step by step over time.

Start with Water Comfort

Before a child can learn swimming skills, they need to feel comfortable being in the water. This can begin with splashing, playing on the steps, holding the wall, and getting used to water on the face.

Keep Early Practice Positive

Young children learn best when water time feels calm and encouraging. Avoid forcing skills too quickly. Short, positive practice sessions often work better than long, stressful ones.

Teach Floating First

Floating is one of the most important water safety skills a child can learn. A child who can float is already building an essential foundation for safer swimming.

Read our guide on how to teach a child to float for a closer look at this skill.

Practice Kicking and Movement

Once a child is comfortable in the water, they can begin practicing simple kicking and body movement. Holding the wall, using support from a parent, or practicing short glides can help children begin feeling how their body moves through water.

Build Confidence Gradually

Children do not learn to swim all at once. Confidence develops little by little. Celebrate small progress, such as putting their face in the water, floating for a few seconds, or kicking to the wall.

Use Safe, Supervised Practice

Even if a child is making progress, active adult supervision is always necessary around water. Swimming ability does not replace close supervision.

For more safety guidance, read how to prevent child drowning.

Know When Professional Lessons Help

Many families begin with casual practice, but professional swim lessons can help children build skills more efficiently and safely. Experienced instructors know how to teach water confidence, floating, kicking, and safety habits in a structured way.
