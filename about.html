About WaterWiseKids

America's free national hub for water safety, swim education, and aquatics careers

Our Mission

Drowning is the second leading cause of accidental death in children under 14 — and most of it is preventable. Our mission is to make water safety knowledge and quality swim education accessible to every family in America, completely free. No paywalls. No subscriptions. Just trusted information when families need it most.

Our Story

WaterWiseKids was built by aquatics professionals who saw a critical problem: families searching for water safety information were getting scattered, unreliable results. Parents couldn't easily find quality swim programs in their area. Teens interested in aquatics careers had no single place to start. And drowning prevention resources were buried behind paywalls or written in clinical language no parent could follow.

WaterWiseKids.com was built by a nationwide team of aquatics professionals, certified swim instructors, and water safety advocates who saw the need for a comprehensive, national water safety resource. We've combined decades of hands-on aquatics expertise with technology to serve families across all 50 states.

Our founding belief — that every person deserves the right to be water-safe — drives everything we publish here. We believe no child should drown because their family didn't have access to the right information or couldn't find a quality program nearby.

Today, WaterWiseKids serves hundreds of thousands of parents, educators, and aquatics professionals every year across all 50 states.

Our Commitment to Safety

Every article we publish goes through review by certified aquatics professionals. We reference official guidelines from the American Red Cross, CDC, USA Swimming, and the World Health Organization. We update content immediately when safety standards change.

We take drowning prevention seriously because every statistic represents a child and a family. Drowning kills roughly 800 children in the U.S. each year — the vast majority could have been prevented with proper water safety education and swimming ability. That urgency drives everything we publish.

Our Editorial Standards

✓
Accuracy First. All safety content is reviewed by certified aquatics professionals before publication.
✓
Always Current. We monitor changes to safety guidelines and update content immediately when standards evolve.
✓
Transparent. We disclose author credentials and source all statistics to authoritative organizations.
✓

✓

✓
Independent. Editorial content is never influenced by advertisers or listed swim schools.
