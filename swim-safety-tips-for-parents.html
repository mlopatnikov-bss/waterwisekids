Swim Safety Tips for Parents

Parents play the biggest role in helping children stay safe around water. Good swim safety habits reduce risk and teach children to respect water from an early age.

Use Active Supervision

Always keep your eyes on children when they are near water. Active supervision means staying close, staying alert, and avoiding distractions like phones or conversations.

Use Layers of Protection

Pool fences, self-latching gates, alarms, and covers all help reduce access to water when children are not being supervised.

Enroll Kids in Swim Lessons

Swim lessons help children become more comfortable in the water and build important skills like floating and reaching the wall.

Read more in how to prevent child drowning.

Teach Pool Rules Early

Children should learn not to run near the pool, never enter water without permission, and always swim with an adult present.

Our article on pool safety rules for kids goes deeper on this topic.

Be Prepared for Emergencies

Parents and caregivers should know basic rescue principles and consider CPR training. Quick action matters in water emergencies.

Model Calm, Safe Behavior

Children often copy what they see. Calm and consistent safety habits from adults help children take water rules seriously.
