What to Look For in a Swim School

The six qualities that matter most when choosing swim lessons for your child.

Safety Certifications

Instructors should hold current certifications in water safety, CPR, and first aid — plus specialized training for the ages they teach.

Small Class Sizes

Look for 4:1 or lower ratios for children under 4, and no more than 6:1 for older kids. Smaller groups mean faster progress.

Survival Skills Focus

The best programs teach water survival — back float, roll-to-breathe, treading — before stroke technique.

Age-Appropriate Progression

Quality schools structure levels around developmental milestones, not just calendar age.

Parent Communication

Regular progress updates and honest conversations when a child is struggling are signs of a serious program.

Warm Water Facilities

Pool temperature matters — especially for young children. Warm water (88–92°F) keeps small bodies focused on learning.
