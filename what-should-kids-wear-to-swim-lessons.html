What Should Kids Wear to Swim Lessons?

Parents often wonder what children should wear to swim lessons. The good news is that the answer is usually simple. Most kids need a comfortable swimsuit, a towel, and any program-specific items required by the swim school.

A Comfortable Swimsuit Comes First

Children should wear a swimsuit that fits well and allows easy movement. It should not be too loose or uncomfortable. A good fit helps kids move more freely in the water.

Swim Diapers for Babies and Some Toddlers

Infants and toddlers who are not fully potty trained usually need approved swim diapers. Some programs may also require a reusable swim diaper over a disposable one, so it is worth checking your swim school's policy before class.

Goggles May Be Helpful

Some children like wearing goggles because they feel more comfortable putting their face in the water. Other programs prefer children to spend part of the lesson without them. It depends on the class structure and the child’s comfort level.

Bring a Towel and Dry Clothes

After swim lessons, children will want a towel and comfortable dry clothes. Younger children may also benefit from having an extra bag for wet items.

Keep It Simple

Parents sometimes overpack for swim lessons. In most cases, children do best with the basics: swimsuit, towel, swim diaper if needed, and any required gear from the program.

First Lessons Can Feel New

What a child wears is only one part of being ready. It also helps to know what to expect during the class.
