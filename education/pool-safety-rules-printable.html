10 Pool Safety Rules
Post, review, and enforce before every swim
📋

Review these rules with ALL swimmers and guests before entering the water. Consistency saves lives.

1

Never Swim Alone

Always use the buddy system. No exceptions — regardless of swimming ability.

2

An Adult Watches the Water

One adult is the designated Water Watcher — no phone, no food, just watching.

3

Ask Permission to Enter

Children must ask an adult before getting in. No exceptions.

4

No Running or Horseplay

Wet decks are slippery. Walk calmly. No diving in non-designated areas.

5

Respect "Stop" — Always

If anyone says stop, stop immediately. No dunking, no holding others underwater.

6

No Glass at the Pool

No glass containers of any kind. Plastic cups and bottles only.

7

Know Depth — Stay in Your Zone

Weaker swimmers stay in shallow water only. Know where deep end begins.

8

Tell an Adult When You Exit

Always tell an adult before leaving the pool or pool area.

9

Respect Safety Equipment

Don't move or play with life rings, throw bags, or rescue equipment.

10

Get Help Immediately

If someone is in trouble — shout, throw a flotation device, call 911. Act fast.

🆘

If You Suspect Drowning

Shout for the Water Watcher · Throw something that floats · Call 911 immediately · Do NOT enter the water unless trained · Begin CPR if swimmer is unresponsive and not breathing

Designate a Water Watcher Before Every Swim

The Water Watcher's only job is watching the water — no phone, no food, no distractions. Rotate every 15–20 minutes to stay alert.
