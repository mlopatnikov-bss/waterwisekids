## Business Model Overview

ISR is not a traditional swim school franchise. It is a **certifying body and curriculum licensor** — ISR trains independent instructors who operate under the ISR brand and method. Founded in 1966 by Dr. Harvey Barnett after witnessing a neighbor's child drown. The model is entirely built around private, one-on-one instruction using a proprietary survival-swim methodology.

- **Ages served:** 6 months to 6 years
- **Lesson format:** Private 1:1 only, 10 minutes per session, 5 days/week (Mon–Fri)
- **Program length:** 4–6 weeks (intensive daily commitment)
- **Lesson count:** ~20–30 sessions total
- **Pricing:** $75–$150/week depending on location; full program typically $1,050–$1,200 + $105 registration fee (new students)
- **Instructor training:** 60+ hours hands-on water training with a Certified ISR Master Instructor + behavioral science coursework
- **Scale:** Claims 8+ million lessons delivered, 800+ documented "survival stories"
- **Geography:** U.S. only; instructor locator on site

## Curriculum: ISR Self-Rescue Program

ISR teaches two distinct skill levels based on child's age and developmental stage:

### Level 1: Rollback to Float (infants 6–18 months)
- Single skill: child falls/is placed in water → rolls onto back → floats and breathes → awaits rescue
- Parents do NOT enter the pool during lessons (until the final week)
- Child is gradually introduced to submersion, independent floating
- Signature element: **clothed water entry** — children practice in street clothes including winter layers, because "most drownings happen when children are dressed for the day"

### Level 2: Swim-Float-Swim (toddlers ~18 months to 6 years)
- Child falls in → swims a short distance → rolls to back → floats and rests → rolls back to stomach → swims again → repeats until reaching safety
- Sequence continues until child reaches steps, wall, or shoreline
- Older children also learn to remove clothing in water

### Refreshers
ISR strongly recommends 6-month refresher sessions ("skills decay as children grow and bodies change"). Refresher sessions are shorter — typically 1–2 weeks. This creates a recurring revenue model for independent instructors.

## Marketing Claims (and Where They're Weak)

ISR's marketing is aggressive and heavily defensive. Their Splash Zone blog is predominantly reactive — addressing controversy, lawsuits, criticism, and competitor comparisons. Key claims they make:

1. **"Safest and most effective"** survival swimming program — no independent peer-reviewed study supports this claim
2. **"800 documented survival stories"** — out of 8M+ lessons; critics note that's 0.01% and some stories don't clearly demonstrate ISR skill use
3. **"Most thoroughly researched"** infant swim program — ISR cites no independent third-party research; all references are internal
4. **"Children can save themselves"** — the AAP explicitly does not endorse this framing for children under age 1
5. **Lawsuit page** (published March 2024): ISR preemptively addresses legal challenges to defend instructor certification standards — suggests ongoing liability exposure

### What the AAP Actually Says
- AAP does NOT recommend formal swim programs for children under 12 months
- AAP (2010 policy update) says swim lessons *may* reduce drowning risk for children ages 1–4, but did not endorse ISR specifically
- AAP confirms children under 4–5 are generally not developmentally ready for full self-rescue (the motor pattern ISR claims to teach)
- Science-Based Medicine published a critique calling infant survival swim programs "still unproven"

ISR's "Splash Zone" blog covers:

- Responses to controversy and lawsuits
- Pool chemical safety (answering parent fears about chlorine)
- Comparing ISR to traditional lessons (self-serving comparisons)

### Critical Content Gaps on ISR's Own Site
1. **Zero independent research citations** — all scientific-sounding claims are self-referential
2. **No skill retention data** — recommends 6-month refreshers but publishes no explanation of skill decay timelines
3. **No developmental science explanation** — never explains WHY a 6-month-old can learn rollback float or what the neuroscience says
4. **No parent preparation guide** — parents are not told what the daily 10-minute format is like emotionally or logistically
5. **No comparison of survival swim methods** (ISR vs. YMCA's Jump-Push-Turn-Grab vs. BSS vs. Sunsational Swim Sequence)
6. **No honest "is ISR right for my child?" framework** — all content assumes ISR is universally correct
7. **No open water / lake / beach content** — all pool-focused despite drownings happening in all water environments
