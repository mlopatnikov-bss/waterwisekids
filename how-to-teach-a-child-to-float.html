How to Teach a Child to Float

Floating is one of the most important early water safety skills children can learn. When a child understands how to float, they gain confidence and a basic survival skill that can help keep them safer in the water.

Start With Comfort in the Water

Before practicing floating, children should feel relaxed in the water. Encourage gentle play, splashing, and simple activities that build trust and comfort.

Support the Head and Back

When helping a child learn to float, support their head and back while encouraging them to relax their body. Many children tense up at first, which makes floating harder.

Practice Short Sessions

Short, positive practice sessions work best. A few minutes at a time helps children gain confidence without feeling overwhelmed.

Consider Professional Swim Lessons

Professional swim instructors use structured teaching techniques that help children learn floating and other survival skills safely.
