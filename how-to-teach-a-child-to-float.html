How to Teach a Child to Float

Floating is one of the most important water survival skills a child can learn. A child who can float on their back has a much better chance of staying calm and staying above water if they accidentally enter a pool or body of water.

Why Floating Is an Essential Skill

Many parents focus first on swimming strokes, but floating often comes before strong swimming. Floating helps children learn balance in the water, relax their bodies, and build confidence.

Start With Comfort in the Water

Before practicing floating, children should feel comfortable being in the water. Let them splash, play, and move around while holding the pool edge or standing in shallow water.

Practice Leaning Back Slowly

Floating on the back can feel unfamiliar to children because their face points upward. Start by holding your child gently under their shoulders and encouraging them to lean back while keeping their ears in the water.

Encourage a Relaxed Body Position

Floating works best when the body stays relaxed. Encourage children to stretch their arms and legs slightly outward and take slow breaths.

Use Short Practice Sessions

Children learn best through short, positive experiences. Practice floating for a few seconds at a time and gradually extend the duration as your child becomes more comfortable.

Consider Structured Swim Lessons

While parents can introduce floating, structured swim lessons often help children learn faster. Instructors use specific techniques designed to teach water confidence and safety skills.
